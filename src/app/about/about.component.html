About ASEEM

Working for the rights and entitlements of marginalized communities

Vision

ASEEM envisions a just and inclusive society where every individual lives with dignity and equal rights.

Mission

We empower marginalized communities, especially Muslims, through advocacy, research, and access to welfare schemes, striving for socio-economic empowerment that leads to equity and justice.

Who We Are

ASEEM is a registered NGO based in Hyderabad, India comprising a group of activists, researchers, lawyers, and social workers who work for the rights and entitlements of the marginalized communities. We believe that every person deserves dignity, justice, and equality, regardless of their caste, class, gender, religion, or ethnicity.

We aim for socio-economic empowerment of minority communities by helping them to apply for welfare schemes and programmes, providing them with legal aid, training, and advocacy. We also conduct action research and documentation on the issues affecting the poor and marginalized sections of the society.

We use various strategies such as advocacy, conducting workshops, orientation programs, leadership development and filing public interest litigation (PIL) to enable the marginalized people to access their constitutional rights and entitlements. We are committed to creating a more inclusive and democratic society where everyone can live with human dignity.

Stark Developmental Disparities

The Sachar Committee Report highlighted that the Muslim Community in India lags behind in all development indicators and in many aspects is more backward than the Dalits:

17%
Children in the community complete matriculation
8%
Muslims in urban areas are employed in the organized sector
4.6%
Of the total loans given by banks go to the Muslim Community

The most recent Sachar Commission Report published in 2016 further reiterates the backwardness of the community.
