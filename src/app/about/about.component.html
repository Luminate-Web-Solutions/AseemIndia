About Us

ASEEM engages in advocacy / legal interventions, action research and empowerment of grass roots organizations and social workers in the Muslim Community to enable marginalized community to access their Constitutional Rights and Entitlements.

Call to Action

There are numerous Muslim Charities which provide relief, succour and support to millions of poor from within the community. Their rationale is that philanthropy and charity are central tenets of Islam. However, a closer inspection reveals that often well meaning philanthropy has created a community that is dependent on hand-outs and alienated it from the mainstream. Thus, the movement from charity towards development and empowerment which transforms beneficiaries into stakeholders in the process of development seems to have completely bypassed the community. This becomes a major reason why the community seldom accesses government development programmes resulting in movement away from the national mainstream.

Stark Developmental Disparities: Sachar Committee Highlights Muslim Backwardness in India

Muslim Community in India lags behind in all development indicators and in many aspects is more backward than the dalits. The Sachar Committee has pointed this out very starkly. According to its report:

17%

children in the community complete matriculation

8%

Muslims in urban areas are employed in the organized sector

4.6%

of the total loans given by banks go to the Muslim Community

The most recent Sachar Commission Report published in 2016, further reiterates the backwardness of the community.

Challenges

Restricted Access to Government Programs

Complex processes and procedures severely restrict the community's access to government programmes and schemes. This results in severe under-utilization of budgets allocated for development of Minority Communities.

Limited Banking Services for Muslim Entrepreneurs and Artisans

Most Muslim are either micro entrepreneurs or artisans. Their access to banking services is severely restricted resulting in exploitation by unorganized money lenders from whom they borrow to meet their working capital needs.

Dependence on Charity Hindering Community Development

Being perennially dependent on doles and charity, the community's growth is stunted. Dependence on charitable programs is a relationship. Therefore the real issues of the community do not find adequate representation in the government and other policy formulation platforms.

What we do?

ASEEM works with volunteers engaged with grass-root organizations in the Muslim Community. These volunteers to be leaders/workers dedicated to trigger movements within the community towards accessing their constitutional rights to social and economic development. We realize that the impact of this kind of an intervention will be limited if it remains only a time-sustained and mere-governmental effort and visualize the community and make them productive citizens.
