About ASEEM

Why the need?

There are numerous Muslim Charities which provide relief, succour and support to millions of poor from within the community. Their rationale is that philanthropy and charity are central tenets of Islam. However, a closer inspection reveals that often well meaning philanthropy has created a community that is dependent on hand-outs and alienated it from the mainstream.

Case Studies

Not just one but many other success stories remain. A story shown here

Mr Karaar Ali voice expresses a blend of happiness and regret as he narrates how our timely intervention enabled him to get a scholarship for his daughter Syeda Mehabeen Fatima, studying in Grade-7, Fountainhead School. He is grateful that our staff contacted him and explained the process of application for scholarship as well as facilitated the process for him. He found it difficult to educate all his children- a son and two daughters but this scholarship ensured that the financial burden of educating one of his daughters is lifted from his shoulders. He plans to apply for the second daughter's scholarship with our help. His regret is that if he had known of this scheme and of us, his older son would not have had to discontinue school and take up a job.

- Mir Karaar Ali
