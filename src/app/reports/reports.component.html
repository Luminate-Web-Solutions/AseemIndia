Philanthropy in the Muslim Community - From Charity to Social Justice

An effort to understand how philanthropy works in the Muslim Community, the impact it has on the lives of poor of the community and how it can move from mere charity towards promoting social justice. The book is a result of exhaustive interactions between several groups who are stakeholders in Muslim Philanthropy. It is an attempt to present how Philanthropy is viewed by different sections of the Muslim Society in Hyderabad and how it compares with the general trend across other Muslim countries.

ASEEM - HUL Study Report

The Report examines the efforts of ASEEM in empowering Muslim Community to claim their rights and entitlements by accessing government programmes and schemes. ASEEM's organization structure, its field of operations, its strategies and challenges that ASEEM faces in terms of corruption, lack of awareness and technology failures are discussed in detail. The report was an outcome of a collaborative effort of ASEEM with Hyderabad Urban Lab.

Booklet on CAA, NPR, NRC in India

The booklet was brought out after the Citizenship Amendment Act, 2019 was enacted by the Parliament. It explains how this Act alters the very idea of India as a secular democratic republic and has the potential to destroy the rich diversity of the country which makes India the most unique nation in the world. The Act also affects census operations and the consequent updation of National Population Register (NPR) which was being linked to the creation of the National Register of Indian Citizens (NRC).

Minority Rights in India and Legal Resources for Social Action

Discusses in detail the origination of rights and entitlements of Marginalized Communities. It elaborates upon how recognition and protection of minority rights under international law began with the League of Nations which adopted several "Minority Treaties." It goes on to explain in detail how the Constitution of India recognizes and protects rights of all citizens and the legal mechanisms it created to protect rights of marginalized and minority communities.

ASEEM Report 2021-2022

Muslim Community in India lags behind in all development indicators and in many aspects is more backward than the dalits. The Sachar Committee has pointed this out very clearly.

According to its report:

  • Only 17% children in the community complete matriculation
  • Only 8% Muslims in urban areas are employed in the organized sector
  • Only 46% of the total loans given by banks go to the Muslim Community

ASEEM Report 2016-2017

Marginalised communities are not integrated into the nation's mainstream despite the many schemes and benefits that the government has to offer because very often well meaning philanthropy from within the community creates a content unproductive recipient community that is dependent on hand-outs and alienated from the mainstream. A small group of like-minded people came forward to create awareness of and access to government schemes and benefits among the marginalised communities. Syed Naqi M.Sc and Al-Asghar arrived with considerable experience of having worked with artisans and workers for two decades spearheaded this intervention. Their logic was simple and clear: the government has funds and it has a constitutional obligation towards its citizens.

ASEEM Report 2015-2021

From 2015-2021 we focused on enabling poor Muslims in Hyderabad to access government programmes and funds. We hand held them through the entire process of preparing applications, opening bank accounts, filing applications and follow up till they start receiving funds in their bank accounts. In order to scale up community impact we strategically shifted focus in 2016 from service delivery to facilitating and training Grass Roots Organizations in accessing government programmes and funds while we actively engaged in advocacy and social audit (research) of government programmes meant for minority community for better and timely implementation.
